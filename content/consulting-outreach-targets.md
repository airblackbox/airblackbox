## Outreach Strategy Notes

**These are NOT like the open-source outreach emails.** Key differences:

1. **You can't scan their code** - consulting firms don't have a single open-source repo to scan. The pitch is about the tool, not their gaps.
2. **The buyer is different** - they're buying tools to use WITH clients, not for themselves.
3. **The value prop is different** - "Give your consultants a tool that scans client AI codebases in 30 seconds and generates compliance reports they can charge for."
4. **Enterprise pricing matters** - the $299 VSP might be too cheap for them to care. Think about a consulting/partner tier: unlimited scans, white-label reports, bulk licensing.
5. **LinkedIn might work better** - these people are more reachable via warm LinkedIn messages than cold email. Consider connecting first, then messaging.

**Recommended approach:**
- Start with Gregor Strojin (Deloitte CE) - most directly relevant, confirmed email, mid-senior enough to actually read cold email
- Then Henning Soller (McKinsey) - Frankfurt-based, writes about AI compliance, pattern-based email
- Arnab Chakraborty (Accenture) last - most senior, hardest to reach, but biggest potential deal
- Consider LinkedIn outreach in parallel for all three

**What to lead with:**
- "Open-source EU AI Act compliance scanner, 1,700+ installs/month"
- "Scans Python AI codebases and maps technical patterns to Articles 9-15"
- "Everything runs locally, no client data leaves their machine" (this is huge for consulting - client confidentiality)
- "Could your compliance teams use this in client engagements?"
